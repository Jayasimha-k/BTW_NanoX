How The Analysis Works
01
Stefan-Boltzmann Law
Each of the 64 pixels has its temperature read by the AMG8833 sensor. The radiated power per pixel is computed as Q = ε × σ × A × T⁴ where ε=0.95 (emissivity), σ=5.67×10⁻⁸ (Stefan-Boltzmann constant), A=1cm² (pixel area), and T is temperature in Kelvin.
02
Excess Power Calculation
Baseline power at 25°C ambient is subtracted from each pixel's measured radiated power. Any positive delta is "wasted" thermal energy — heat escaping the HVAC vent that should have stayed in the system or been converted to useful work.
03
Energy Waste Index
EWI is a 0–100 normalised score. 0 means all pixels are at baseline (25°C, no waste). 100 means all 64 pixels are at 60°C. It gives a single number that captures how inefficient the system is at any moment.
04
Cumulative Waste & CO₂
Excess power is integrated over time (ΔWh = P × Δt) to give cumulative wasted watt-hours. CO₂ is calculated using India's grid emission factor of 0.82 kg per kWh from CEA 2023. Cost uses the average Indian electricity tariff of ₹7/kWh.
